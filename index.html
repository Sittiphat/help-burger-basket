What Happened

On March 26, 2026, the owners of Burger Basket Monrovia, Pan and Watt, were the victims of a targeted home burglary that resulted in the loss of their entire life savings. Their apartment—just a short walk from the restaurant—was broken into by a group of 3–4 perpetrators and a getaway driver using a light gray Nissan Rogue SUV with stolen plates (9FIDO94).

An older man in a white hat first scouted the home by forcefully knocking on the windows. Minutes later, two younger men broke in through a small bathroom window (shown in the walkthrough video). Once inside, they manhandled the family’s three dogs (who thankfully were not seriously hurt) by putting the smaller white dog in one room, the other smaller black dog in another room and went straight to the bedroom, stealing approximately $20,000 in valuables and around $140,000 in life savings—money earned over decades of hard work in the restaurant industry and Burger Basket.

The emotional toll has been severe. Shortly after the burglary, Vorawat was hospitalized with a stress‑induced condition, and the family temporarily closed Burger Basket to recover. Despite this devastation, the Monrovia community has shown overwhelming kindness—visiting the restaurant, offering support, and helping spread the word.

This website exists to help the community identify the suspects, support the family, and share verified information. Your help truly matters.

Interesting Observations

Observations drawn from frame-by-frame analysis of the Ring camera footage and still images. Analysis performed by Claude AI and the community. Contribute to the discussion forum located below, so that more clues can be added.

Note: These are analytical observations only — not verified conclusions. If you notice something additional in the footage, share it in the Community Forum or contact Jesse at (814) 406-9539 or the Monrovia Police Department.

  • At least 30 minutes of patient pre-entry surveillance. Ring footage shows the scout in the white hat outside the bathroom window at 4:54 PM. The group did not enter until after 5:22 PM. This level of patience — over 30 minutes — is not typical of opportunistic crime. The crew waited long enough to be confident the home would stay empty.

  • Coordinated multi-role operation with at least four distinct participants. Video evidence confirms: (1) a scout in a white hat who surveilled the exterior beforehand, (2) at least two masked perpetrators who ransacked the interior simultaneously, and (3) a dedicated getaway driver who stayed parked in the Nissan Rogue outside the entire time. A mysterious fourth party — a woman — was on speakerphone with two suspects inside. Her role (lookout, coordinator, or other) is unknown.

  • Gloves worn throughout — came prepared to leave no trace. Interior Ring footage clearly shows at least one perpetrator wearing bright red gloves while ransacking the living room. This was a deliberate choice to avoid fingerprint evidence and rules out any improvised or opportunistic crime.

  • Stolen license plates sourced before arriving. The Nissan Rogue was already fitted with stolen plate 9FIDO94 when it arrived on scene. This required a separate, prior criminal act — sourcing a stolen plate — and confirms the burglary was planned well in advance, not impulsive.

  • Camera awareness — hallway camera appears deliberately blocked. At 5:28 PM, the hallway Ring camera view becomes obstructed. The abrupt angle change suggests a suspect noticed and moved or covered the camera. This shows awareness of Ring cameras, consistent with a crew that has done this before.

  • Targeted the room with the most visible valuables The $140,000 was stored in a lockbox inside the master bedroom, in close proximity to other valuables and jewelry. The burglars’ decision to concentrate their efforts on the room most likely to contain high-value items—rather than indiscriminately ransacking the first space they encountered—along with the speed and efficiency of the search, suggests a level of experience and intent. This pattern is consistent with individuals familiar with residential burglaries. It also raises the possibility that they had prior knowledge of the home’s layout and the occupants’ schedules, indicating deliberate planning. Such knowledge could have been obtained through surveillance or, potentially, through information shared by someone with proximity to the family, such as a neighbor, customer, or acquaintance. Additionally, the home’s close proximity to Burger Basket increases the likelihood that the location may have been observed and specifically targeted in advance.

  • Spanish-language audio captured on multiple Ring clips. Several Ring recordings contain audible Spanish-language speech, likely from the perpetrators. The content of these conversations could contain critical information — meeting points, names, instructions. Community members who speak Spanish are strongly encouraged to listen and transcribe what they hear in the Community Forum.

  • 3 Family dogs were present. At first, they barked at the scout in the white hat and reacted defensively when the two masked men emerged from the bathroom. The intruders attempted to calm the dogs by repeatedly opening the bathroom door and speaking to them in a gentle tone. Winchester, the smaller black dog, showed the strongest defensive reaction, while the white dog, Lucky, barked mostly out of fear. The German Shepherd remained the most composed. Because of this, the burglars physically moved the black and white dogs (Winchester and Lucky) into another room while leaving the German Shepherd (Happy) where he was.
