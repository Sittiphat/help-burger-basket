What Happened

Pan and Watt are Thai immigrant owners of Burger Basket, a beloved mom-and-pop restaurant that has served the Monrovia community since 1963 — back when a burger and fries cost just $0.65. The couple purchased the restaurant about seven years ago and were finally looking forward to retiring within the next 5 to 7 years.

On Thursday, March 26, 2026, while Pan and Watt were both at work running the restaurant, a professional burglary crew targeted their nearby Monrovia home. The operation had been planned in advance: the group began surveilling the property as early as 4:54 PM, with a scout in a distinctive white hat crouching outside the bathroom window and peering through the glass to confirm the house was empty.

After over 30 minutes of surveillance, the crew entered through the small bathroom window — visibly forced open as shown in the post-incident walkthrough footage. A getaway driver stayed parked in a light gray Nissan Rogue SUV (fitted with stolen license plate 9FIDO94) outside the entire time, ready to flee at a moment's notice.

Inside, at least two perpetrators dressed in all-black clothing and face masks moved swiftly through the home. Ring cameras captured them ransacking the bedroom and living room simultaneously. At one point, two masked suspects are visible on camera talking to a mysterious woman on speakerphone — her voice and role in the crime remain unknown. At least one perpetrator wore bright red gloves to avoid leaving fingerprints.

The perpetrators located and stole a lockbox containing approximately $140,000 in cash — 30 years of life savings Pan and Watt had set aside for retirement. They also took an estimated $20,000 in valuables and irreplaceable family heirlooms passed down through generations. The family's dogs — a German Shepherd and a Corgi — were present in the home throughout.

"We don't have much time left already — we're not young anymore," Pan said. "That money was for my retirement. I've been working for 30 years."

Their son Jesse explains that, like many in the older Asian immigrant community, his parents kept their savings in cash at home — the way they knew how to trust. Shortly after the robbery, Watt was hospitalized due to stress-related illness, forcing Burger Basket to close temporarily.

When the restaurant briefly reopened, the community response was overwhelming. Customers flooded in just to show support. "So many people came to buy more burgers," Jesse said. "It made my dad cry — and I've never seen my dad cry before. He cried with tears of joy."

The Monrovia Police Department is actively investigating. If you recognize anyone in the videos or photos below, please contact MPD immediately.

Interesting Observations

Observations drawn from frame-by-frame analysis of the Ring camera footage and still images. Analysis performed by Claude AI and the community.

Note: These are analytical observations only — not verified conclusions. If you notice something additional in the footage, share it in the Community Forum or contact Jesse at (814) 406-9539 or the Monrovia Police Department.

  • At least 30 minutes of patient pre-entry surveillance. Ring footage shows the scout in the white hat outside the bathroom window at 4:54 PM. The group did not enter until after 5:22 PM. This level of patience — over 30 minutes — is not typical of opportunistic crime. The crew waited long enough to be confident the home would stay empty.

  • Coordinated multi-role operation with at least four distinct participants. Video evidence confirms: (1) a scout in a white hat who surveilled the exterior beforehand, (2) at least two masked perpetrators who ransacked the interior simultaneously, and (3) a dedicated getaway driver who stayed parked in the Nissan Rogue outside the entire time. A mysterious fourth party — a woman — was on speakerphone with two suspects inside. Her role (lookout, coordinator, or other) is unknown.

  • Gloves worn throughout — came prepared to leave no trace. Interior Ring footage clearly shows at least one perpetrator wearing bright red gloves while ransacking the living room. This was a deliberate choice to avoid fingerprint evidence and rules out any improvised or opportunistic crime.

  • Stolen license plates sourced before arriving. The Nissan Rogue was already fitted with stolen plate 9FIDO94 when it arrived on scene. This required a separate, prior criminal act — sourcing a stolen plate — and confirms the burglary was planned well in advance, not impulsive.

  • Camera awareness — hallway camera appears deliberately blocked. At 5:28 PM, the hallway Ring camera view becomes obstructed. The abrupt angle change suggests a suspect noticed and moved or covered the camera. This shows awareness of Ring cameras, consistent with a crew that has done this before.

  • Targeted a hidden lockbox — possible prior knowledge or pattern targeting. The $140,000 was stored in a locked cash box inside the home. The perpetrators located and removed it. This suggests either prior knowledge of the home's contents, or that this crew specifically targets households suspected of storing cash savings — a known pattern in burglaries targeting older immigrant families.

  • Spanish-language audio captured on multiple Ring clips. Several Ring recordings contain audible Spanish-language speech, likely from the perpetrators. The content of these conversations could contain critical information — meeting points, names, instructions. Community members who speak Spanish are strongly encouraged to listen and transcribe what they hear in the Community Forum.

  • 3 Family dogs were present. In the beginning, the dogs barked at the scout in the white hat and showed agression when the perpetrators first came out of the bathroom door. The two masked men used descalation tactics such as repeatingly opening the bathroom door and talking sweetly to the dogs. The smaller black dog, Winchester, showed the most aggression at the burglars and the white dog showed the second most aggression, but mostly barked in fear. The German Shepherd was most unmoved, thus, they manhandled the black and white dog into the other room while leaving the German Shepherd alone.
