What Happened

Pan and Watt are the Thai-immigrant owners of Burger Basket, a beloved mom-and-pop restaurant that has served the Monrovia community since 1963 — back when a burger and fries cost just $0.65. The couple purchased the restaurant from friends about seven years ago and were finally looking forward to retiring within the next 5 to 7 years.

On March 26, 2026, that dream was devastated. While Pan and Watt were at work, a group of thieves targeted their nearby home. One suspect knocked on the front door first; when no one answered, others broke in through a small window. They manhandled the family's dogs, ransacked the house, and stole approximately $140,000 in cash from a lockbox — money the couple had painstakingly saved over 30 years of hard work. The intruders also took an estimated $20,000 in valuables and irreplaceable family heirlooms passed down through generations.

Security cameras captured the suspects in the act. They fled in a light gray Nissan Rogue SUV bearing stolen plates (9FIDO94), with a designated getaway driver waiting outside.

"We don't have much time left already — we're not young anymore," Pan said. "That money was for my retirement. I've been working for 30 years."

Their son Jesse explains that, like many in the older Asian immigrant community, his parents kept their savings in cash at home — the way they knew how to trust. Shortly after the robbery, Watt was hospitalized due to stress-related illness, forcing Burger Basket to close temporarily.

When the restaurant briefly reopened, the community response was overwhelming. Customers flooded in just to show support. "So many people came to buy more burgers," Jesse said. "It made my dad cry — and I've never seen my dad cry before. He cried with tears of joy."

The Monrovia Police Department is actively investigating. If you recognize anyone in the videos or photos below, please contact MPD immediately.

Interesting Observations

Observations drawn from evidence footage and still images by Claude AI and the community.

Note: These are analytical observations only — not verified conclusions. If you notice something in the footage, contact Jesse at (814) 406-9539 or the Monrovia Police Department.

  • Gloves worn during the break-in — premeditated preparation. Interior Ring camera footage clearly shows at least one perpetrator wearing bright red gloves while ransacking the bedroom. This strongly suggests the group came prepared to avoid leaving fingerprints, ruling out an impulsive or opportunistic crime.

  • Coordinated, multi-role operation with at least 4 participants. Evidence confirms distinct roles: (1) a scout in a white hat who arrived beforehand to case the property, (2) a suspect who knocked on the front door to confirm the home was empty, (3) at least two perpetrators who entered through the opened window and ransacked interior rooms simultaneously, and (4) a dedicated getaway driver who remained in the Nissan Rogue at all times.

  • Stolen license plates arranged in advance. The getaway Nissan Rogue was fitted with stolen plates (9FIDO94) before arriving at the scene. Sourcing stolen plates requires separate planning effort, further confirming this was a deliberate, premeditated operation.

  • Group targeted a locked cash box — possible inside knowledge. The $140,000 was stored in a lockbox, not left in the open. The fact that the perpetrators located and removed it during the ransacking suggests either prior knowledge of the home's layout and storage, or that this group has a pattern of targeting cash savings specifically.

  • Scout surveilled from inside a vehicle — classic casing technique. Still images show the man in the white hat observing the property while seated inside or near a vehicle. Casing a target from a vehicle is less conspicuous than loitering on foot and allows for rapid departure, consistent with an experienced, organized group.
