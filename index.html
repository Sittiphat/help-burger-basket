What Happened

Pan and Watt are the Thai-immigrant owners of Burger Basket, a beloved mom-and-pop restaurant that has served the Monrovia community since 1963 — back when a burger and fries cost just $0.65. The couple purchased the restaurant from friends about seven years ago and were finally looking forward to retiring within the next 5 to 7 years.

On March 26, 2026, that dream was devastated. While Pan and Watt were at work, a group of thieves targeted their nearby home. One suspect knocked on the front door first; when no one answered, others broke in through a small window. They manhandled the family's dogs, ransacked the house, and stole approximately $140,000 in cash from a lockbox — money the couple had painstakingly saved over 30 years of hard work. The intruders also took an estimated $20,000 in valuables and irreplaceable family heirlooms passed down through generations.

Security cameras captured the suspects in the act. They fled in a light gray Nissan Rogue SUV bearing stolen plates (9FIDO94), with a designated getaway driver waiting outside.

"We don't have much time left already — we're not young anymore," Pan said. "That money was for my retirement. I've been working for 30 years."

Their son Jesse explains that, like many in the older Asian immigrant community, his parents kept their savings in cash at home — the way they knew how to trust. Shortly after the robbery, Watt was hospitalized due to stress-related illness, forcing Burger Basket to close temporarily.

When the restaurant briefly reopened, the community response was overwhelming. Customers flooded in just to show support. "So many people came to buy more burgers," Jesse said. "It made my dad cry — and I've never seen my dad cry before. He cried with tears of joy."

The Monrovia Police Department is actively investigating. If you recognize anyone in the videos or photos below, please contact MPD immediately.
