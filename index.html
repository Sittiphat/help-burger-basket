The Full Story

What Happened

Pan and Watt are Thai immigrant owners of Burger Basket, a beloved mom-and-pop restaurant that has served the Monrovia community since 1963 — back when a burger and fries cost just $0.65. The couple purchased the restaurant about seven years ago and were finally looking forward to retiring within the next 5 to 7 years.

On Thursday, March 26, 2026, while Pan and Watt were both at work, a professional burglary crew targeted their nearby Monrovia home. The operation had been planned in advance: the group began surveilling the property as early as 4:54 PM, with a scout in a distinctive white hat crouching outside the bathroom window and peering through the glass to confirm the house was empty.

After over 30 minutes of surveillance, the crew entered through the small bathroom window — visibly forced open as shown in the post-incident walkthrough. A getaway driver stayed parked in a light gray Nissan Rogue SUV (fitted with stolen license plate 9FIDO94) outside the entire time.

Inside, at least two perpetrators in all-black clothing and face masks ransacked the bedroom and living room simultaneously. At one point, two masked suspects are visible on camera talking to a mysterious woman on speakerphone — her voice and role remain unknown. At least one perpetrator wore bright red gloves to avoid leaving fingerprints.

The perpetrators located and stole a lockbox containing approximately $140,000 in cash — 30 years of life savings set aside for retirement. They also took an estimated $20,000 in valuables and irreplaceable family heirlooms passed down through generations.

"We don't have much time left already — we're not young anymore. That money was for my retirement. I've been working for 30 years."

— Pan, co-owner of Burger Basket

Their son Jesse explains that, like many in the older Asian immigrant community, his parents kept their savings in cash at home — the way they knew how to trust. Shortly after the robbery, Watt was hospitalized due to stress-related illness, forcing Burger Basket to close temporarily.

When the restaurant briefly reopened, the community response was overwhelming. Customers flooded in just to show support. "So many people came to buy more burgers," Jesse said. "It made my dad cry — and I've never seen my dad cry before. He cried with tears of joy."

The Monrovia Police Department is actively investigating. If you recognize anyone in the videos or photos below, please contact MPD immediately.

Frame-by-Frame Analysis

Interesting Observations

Drawn from frame-by-frame analysis of Ring camera footage and still images. Analysis by Claude AI and the community.

Analytical observations only — not verified conclusions. Share findings in the Community Forum or contact Jesse at (814) 406-9539.

At least 30 minutes of patient pre-entry surveillance.

Ring footage shows the scout at 4:54 PM. The group didn't enter until after 5:22 PM. This level of patience is not typical of opportunistic crime — they waited long enough to be certain the home would stay empty.

Coordinated multi-role operation with at least four participants.

Evidence confirms: (1) a scout in a white hat for exterior surveillance, (2) at least two masked perpetrators ransacking simultaneously, (3) a dedicated getaway driver who never left the Nissan Rogue, and (4) a mysterious woman on speakerphone whose role is unknown.

Gloves worn throughout — came prepared to leave no trace.

Interior Ring footage clearly shows at least one perpetrator wearing bright red gloves while ransacking the living room. A deliberate choice to avoid fingerprint evidence, ruling out any improvised or opportunistic crime.

Stolen license plates sourced before arriving.

The Nissan Rogue was already fitted with stolen plate 9FIDO94 on arrival. Sourcing a stolen plate is a separate criminal act requiring prior planning — this was not a spontaneous crime.

Camera awareness — hallway camera appears deliberately blocked.

At 5:28 PM the hallway Ring camera becomes obstructed. The abrupt angle change suggests a suspect noticed and covered it — consistent with a crew that has done this before.

Targeted a hidden lockbox — possible prior knowledge.

The $140,000 was in a locked cash box. The perpetrators located and removed it, suggesting either prior knowledge or a pattern of specifically targeting immigrant households known to keep cash savings.

Spanish-language audio captured on multiple Ring clips.

Several recordings contain audible Spanish speech, likely from the perpetrators. Community members who speak Spanish are strongly encouraged to listen and transcribe in the Community Forum.

Three family dogs present — perpetrators used de-escalation tactics.

The dogs showed aggression when the perpetrators first emerged through the bathroom door. The two masked men used de-escalation tactics — repeatedly opening the door, talking sweetly. Winchester (the smaller black dog) showed the most aggression; the white dog mostly barked in fear; the German Shepherd was largely unmoved. The perpetrators manhandled the two smaller dogs into another room while leaving the German Shepherd alone.
